## Overview

*Try to Survive* is a **zombie-themed twin-stick shooter** where players must fend off relentless waves of undead enemies. With fast-paced combat, responsive dual-stick controls, and a survival-focused progression system, the game challenges players to stay alive as long as possible while upgrading their arsenal.  

## Key Features

- **Twin-Stick Controls**: Smooth, responsive movement and aiming for precise shooting and agile dodging.  
- **Zombie Wave System**: Increasingly difficult waves of enemies with varied attack behaviors.  
- **Weapon Variety**: Multiple weapons with unique firing patterns and upgrade potential.  
- **Survival Mechanics**: Resource management for ammo and health creates tension in long runs.  
- **Replayability**: Randomized spawns and scaling difficulty keep each playthrough fresh.  

## Technologies Used

- **Unity (C#)**: Core development engine.  
- **Custom Wave Manager**: Handles dynamic spawning and scaling difficulty.  
- **Git**: Version control and project collaboration.  

## Challenges and Learnings

The biggest challenge was balancing **difficulty progression** while ensuring gameplay remained fun and rewarding. Designing a scalable wave manager required fine-tuning spawn rates, enemy stats, and player resources. This project reinforced my skills in **game balancing, enemy AI, and control responsiveness**.  

## Outcome

The project delivered a **fast-paced survival shooter** that captures the tension of facing endless zombie hordes. The scalable systems and twin-stick control scheme created a solid foundation for replayable and challenging gameplay.
